Accrete is a graph-based compositional engine for real-time generative music in which sound is treated as behaviour rather than as discrete objects. The system is designed to construct, traverse, and transform a network of sounding processes whose relations produce musical form. Instead of selecting and sequencing predefined material, the composer works with an evolving structure in which nodes, connections, and their activation histories shape the unfolding result.

At the core of the system is the idea that each sound unit is a behavioural state. A node does not represent a clip or preset but a generative process with its own internal temporal logic, parameter tendencies, and multichannel spatial configuration. When activated, a node produces sound over time according to its behavioural definition, and its significance lies less in its onset and termination than in how it changes while active. These nodes form the material layer of the system, and they are designed to be reusable, parameterizable, and observable.

Nodes are connected through edges that define possible relations rather than fixed transitions. An edge encodes how one behaviour may lead to another, whether through succession, variation, contrast, or transformation. These relations can also carry parameter influence, allowing characteristics of one node to propagate into another. In this sense, the graph is not only a navigation structure but a space of potential causality, where each connection suggests a different musical consequence.

The graph itself is not static. Accrete treats graph construction as part of the compositional process, allowing nodes and edges to be added, modified, or removed during runtime. Growth models are central to this behaviour. The system supports preferential attachment and other evolving network strategies in which certain nodes accumulate influence while others remain peripheral. Variants that allow edge decay or deletion ensure that the network remains dynamic, with each moment representing a distinct structural state. This shifting topology becomes audible, as changes in connectivity affect how sound behaviours are accessed and combined.

Traversal through the graph is understood as a musical act. Rather than simply walking a predefined path, the system activates nodes according to rules that consider duration, contrast, density, and recent history. Multiple traversal processes may occur simultaneously, allowing layered or polyphonic structures to emerge. Rapid transitions and repeated activations can generate rhythmic material, while sparse traversal can introduce discontinuity and silence. The temporal behaviour of the system is therefore shaped by both the properties of individual nodes and the evolving structure of the graph.

An important component of Accrete is the observer system. Observers are analysis processes attached to active sound behaviours or to the global output. They extract features such as spectral distribution, amplitude, density, or spatial spread, and use these observations to influence the graph. An observer may trigger the activation of a new node, alter the probability of certain transitions, or initiate structural changes such as adding or removing connections. Through this mechanism, the sound itself becomes an active agent in shaping the system, creating a feedback loop between generation and analysis.

Causality is explicitly represented within the system. Every activation, mutation, or structural change is recorded as an event with a traceable origin. Events can reference the node, edge, or observer that caused them, forming chains of dependency that describe how the system evolved over time. This “caused by” structure is not only useful for debugging or analysis but is treated as part of the compositional output. The accumulation of these traces reveals patterns of behaviour and decision that can themselves be interpreted musically.

Spatial sound is integrated at the level of node design. Each behaviour operates in a multichannel context, distributing its parameters across channels rather than being spatialised after synthesis. This approach creates the impression of a sound process unfolding within a higher-dimensional space, where spatial movement arises from the interaction of distributed parameters rather than explicit panning gestures. As the graph evolves and different nodes are activated, these spatial behaviours interact to produce complex and shifting textures.

The system is intended to operate in a live context. The composer can intervene at any point by inserting nodes, modifying connections, changing growth parameters, or influencing traversal behaviour. Control can be exercised through code, messages, or external interfaces, but the emphasis remains on incremental construction and transformation. The graph grows over time, and the act of building it becomes inseparable from the act of performing.

Technically, Accrete is structured as a modular runtime composed of a graph engine, a traversal scheduler, a growth module, an observer framework, and a sound layer. Sound generation is expected to be handled by a real-time audio environment such as SuperCollider, while the graph and control logic may either reside within the same environment or be abstracted into a separate layer. The system is designed to be extensible, allowing new growth models, observer types, and sound behaviours to be introduced without altering the core architecture.
